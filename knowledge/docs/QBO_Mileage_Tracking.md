# QuickBooks Mileage Tracking

## Visual UI Anchors
- **Mileage** (left navigation)
- **Add mileage button**: Log trips
- **Start tracking**: Automatic tracking
- **Vehicle dropdown**: Select vehicle
- **Date, purpose, start/end**: Trip details

## Step-by-Step Workflow (2025)

### Manual Entry
1. Click **Mileage** in left navigation
2. Click **Add mileage**
3. Select **Vehicle**
4. Enter trip details:
   - **Date** of travel
   - **Purpose** of trip
   - **Start** location
   - **End** location
   - **Distance** (auto-calculates if route recognized)
5. Mark as **Billable** if customer will pay
6. Select **Customer** if billable
7. Click **Save**

### Mobile App Automatic Tracking
1. Open QuickBooks mobile app
2. Enable mileage tracking
3. Start trip when beginning drive
4. Stop trip when complete
5. Categorize trip
6. Save

## Why This Matters (Accounting Context)
Mileage tracking:
- Provides tax deduction for business use
- Tracks reimbursable customer expenses
- Maintains required documentation
- Simplifies tax preparation

## Common Error States / Pitfalls

### No purpose recorded
- **What you see:** Miles without business reason
- **Risk:** Disallowed deduction
- **Fix:**
  1. Always enter business purpose
  2. Be specific (client meeting vs business)

### Personal miles included
- **What you see:** Commuting or personal trips
- **Diagnosis:** Not filtering business vs personal
- **Fix:**
  1. Only track business-related miles
  2. Commuting is generally not deductible
