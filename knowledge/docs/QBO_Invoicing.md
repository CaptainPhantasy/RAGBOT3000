# QuickBooks Invoicing

## Visual UI Anchors
- **+ New** → **Invoice**
- **Sales** (left navigation) → **Invoices** tab → **New invoice**
- **Invoice form**: Customer dropdown, Product/Service columns, Terms, Email button
- **Online invoicing toggle**: Enable online payment features
- **Customize button**: Modify invoice appearance

## Step-by-Step Workflow (2025)
1. Click **+ New** → **Invoice** (or Sales → Invoices → New invoice)
2. Select **Customer** from dropdown
3. Enter **Invoice date** and **Due date** (if not set by terms)
4. Add **Products/Services**:
   - Select from dropdown or add new
   - Enter **Quantity** and **Rate**
   - Verify **Amount** calculation
5. Add a **Message** to customer (optional)
6. Review **Total** including tax
7. Click **Save and send** or **Save and close**
8. If sending:
   - Review email template
   - Add additional recipients if needed
   - Click **Send and close**

## Why This Matters (Accounting Context)
Invoices create:
- **Accounts Receivable**: Money customers owe you
- **Sales Revenue**: Recognized income
- **Tax Liability**: Sales tax collected from customers

Proper invoicing ensures timely payment and accurate financial records.

## Common Error States / Pitfalls

### Wrong customer selected
- **What you see:** Invoice appears on wrong customer account
- **Diagnosis:** Customer dropdown selection error
- **Fix:**
  1. Open the invoice
  2. Change customer using dropdown
  3. Save changes

### Incorrect tax application
- **What you see:** Tax not charged or wrong tax rate
- **Diagnosis:** Product/service or customer tax settings incorrect
- **Fix:**
  1. Check product/service tax settings
  2. Verify customer tax exemption status
  3. Update as needed

### Duplicate invoices
- **What you see:** Same invoice number issued twice
- **Diagnosis:** Browser refresh or double-click
- **Fix:**
  1. Find duplicate invoice
  2. Delete it (if unpaid)
  3. If paid, create credit memo or journal entry
