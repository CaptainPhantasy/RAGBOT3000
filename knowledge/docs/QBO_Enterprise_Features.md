# QuickBooks Desktop Enterprise Features (2025)

## Overview
QuickBooks Desktop Enterprise is the most powerful version of QuickBooks, designed for growing and complex businesses. It offers advanced inventory, reporting, and capacity features beyond QuickBooks Online.

## Key 2025 Features

### Customer Prepayments on Sales Orders & Estimates
- Accept customer deposits directly on sales orders
- Track prepayments against final invoice
- Better cash flow visibility
- **Access:** Sales Order form → Prepayment section

### Inventory Turnover Report
- Analyze how quickly inventory sells
- Identify slow-moving items
- Optimize stock levels
- **Access:** Reports → Inventory → Inventory Turnover

### Linked Sales Orders & Purchase Orders
- Create PO directly from Sales Order
- Track fulfillment from sale to delivery
- Better supply chain management
- **Access:** Sales Order → Create Purchase Order

### Enhanced Inventory Receiving
- Receive partial shipments easily
- Track backorders automatically
- Better vendor management
- **Access:** Vendors → Receive Items

### Improved Readability in Reports
- Enhanced font sizes
- Better spacing
- Improved printing
- **Access:** All reports automatically improved

### Enhanced List Search
- Faster search across large lists
- Better filtering options
- Improved results relevance
- **Access:** Search bar in any list window

### Enhanced Security
- Advanced encryption
- Better audit trails
- Improved user permissions
- Role-based access control

## Enterprise vs. Online Comparison

### Capacity
- **Enterprise:** Up to 1 million items (customers, vendors, items)
- **Online:** Limited to 500k transactions per company

### Multi-User
- **Enterprise:** Up to 40 simultaneous users
- **Online:** Varies by subscription (1-25 users)

### Inventory
- **Enterprise:** Advanced inventory with FIFO, serial numbers, lot tracking
- **Online:** Basic inventory (Plus plan and above)

### Pricing
- **Enterprise:** Annual subscription, custom based on users
- **Online:** Monthly subscription tiers

## When to Choose Enterprise
- Manufacturing operations
- Complex inventory needs (serial numbers, lots)
- Large transaction volume
- Need for local data storage
- Complex pricing rules
- Advanced reporting requirements

## Migration Considerations
- Data can be migrated from Online to Enterprise
- Some features may not transfer directly
- Consult with ProAdvisor for complex migrations
- Plan for training on desktop interface
