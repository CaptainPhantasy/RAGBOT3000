## Getting Started in Sales

### The Sales Role in ServiceTitan

Sales professionals and technicians who sell use ServiceTitan to:

- **Build Professional Estimates:** Create detailed, branded quotes
- **Present Options:** Offer good-better-best choices
- **Track Opportunities:** Monitor sales pipeline
- **Close Deals:** Convert estimates to jobs
- **Analyze Performance:** Track metrics and improve
- **Optimize Pricing:** Use dynamic and strategic pricing

### Core Sales Workflows

**Primary Activities:**
- Create estimates for proposed work
- Present options to customers
- Follow up on pending estimates
- Convert sold estimates to jobs
- Track sales metrics and KPIs
- Optimize pricebook and pricing

**Integration Points:**
- **Field Technicians:** Build estimates on-site
- **Office Staff:** Create estimates from calls/visits
- **Dispatch:** Schedule sold estimate work
- **Management:** Review sales performance
- **Accounting:** Process sold estimate invoices

## Pricebook Management

### Understanding the Pricebook

The **Pricebook** is your central catalog of all services, materials, and equipment with associated pricing.

**Pricebook Structure:**
- **Services:** Labor-based offerings (HVAC tune-up, drain cleaning, etc.)
- **Materials:** Parts and supplies (filters, pipes, fittings)
- **Equipment:** Major installations (AC units, water heaters, furnaces)

**Key Attributes:**
- Item name and description
- SKU/Part number
- Cost (what you pay)
- Price (what customer pays)
- Categories and tags
- Business unit assignments
- Active/inactive status

### Creating Pricebook Items

**Adding a Service:**

1. Navigate to **Settings > Pricebook**
2. Click **"Add Service"**
3. Enter details:
   - Name: "HVAC System Tune-Up"
   - Description: Detailed explanation for customers
   - Category: "Maintenance"
   - Business Unit: "HVAC"
4. Set pricing:
   - Standard price
   - Member price (if applicable)
   - Cost (for margin tracking)
5. Add specifications:
   - Estimated duration
   - Required skills/certifications
   - Warranty information
6. **Save**

**Adding Materials/Equipment:**
- Similar process to services
- Include supplier information
- Track inventory (if enabled)
- Set reorder points

### Pricebook Organization

**Categories:**
- Group similar items together
- Makes searching faster
- Examples: "Plumbing Repairs," "HVAC Maintenance," "Electrical Upgrades"

**Tags:**
- Add metadata for advanced filtering
- Examples: "Emergency," "Seasonal," "High-Margin," "Best-Seller"

**Business Units:**
- Assign items to specific units
- HVAC pricebook, Plumbing pricebook, etc.
- Technicians see only relevant items

### Dynamic Pricing

**What is Dynamic Pricing?**
Automatically adjust pricing based on configurable factors:
- Time of day (after-hours premium)
- Day of week (weekend rates)
- Urgency (emergency surcharge)
- Season (peak season pricing)
- Customer type (commercial vs. residential)

**Benefits:**
- Maximize revenue during high-demand periods
- Compensate for overtime and inconvenience
- Competitive pricing during slow periods
- Automated - no manual price adjustments

**Setting Up:**
1. **Settings > Pricebook > Dynamic Pricing**
2. Define rules and multipliers
3. Apply to specific services or categories
4. Test and refine

### Client-Specific Pricing

**Custom Pricing for Specific Customers:**
- Contract rates for commercial clients
- VIP customer discounts
- Property management agreements
- Special negotiated pricing

**How to Set Up:**
1. Open customer record
2. Navigate to **"Pricing" tab**
3. Add custom prices for specific services
4. Set discounts or flat rates
5. **Save**

**Use Cases:**
- Large commercial contracts
- Multi-property agreements
- Long-term partnerships
- Volume discounts

## Creating Estimates

### When to Create Estimates

**Common Scenarios:**
- **Diagnostic Visit:** Found issues during service call
- **Planned Projects:** Customer requests quote for new installation
- **Preventive Recommendations:** Suggest improvements/upgrades
- **Follow-Up:** Customer calls back requesting quote
- **Competitive Bidding:** Customer getting multiple quotes

### Estimate Creation Process

**Step-by-Step (Web Application):**

1. **Navigate to Estimates**
   - Click **"Sales" > "Estimates"** in sidebar
   - OR from job: **"Create Estimate"**

2. **Select Customer & Location**
   - Choose existing customer or create new
   - Select service location
   - Verify contact information

3. **Choose Business Unit**
   - HVAC, Plumbing, Electrical, etc.
   - Determines available pricebook items

4. **Add Services & Materials**
   - **Search pricebook:** Type item name or SKU
   - **Browse categories:** Navigate pricebook structure
   - **Click to add:** Items added to estimate
   - **Adjust quantities:** Modify as needed
   - **Customize descriptions:** Tailor to customer

5. **Organize into Options** (Good-Better-Best)
   - **Option 1 (Good):** Basic solution, meets needs
   - **Option 2 (Better):** Enhanced features, better value
   - **Option 3 (Best):** Premium solution, maximum benefits
   - **Recommended:** Mark your suggested option

6. **Add Photos & Notes**
   - Include photos of issue or equipment
   - Add explanatory notes
   - Attach diagrams or specifications

7. **Review Pricing**
   - Verify all items included
   - Check totals for each option
   - Apply discounts if appropriate
   - Ensure pricing is competitive

8. **Generate Estimate Document**
   - Preview PDF
   - Customize template if needed
   - Add terms and conditions
   - Include warranty information

9. **Save as Draft** (or Present immediately)

### Estimate Options & Packages

**Single Estimate:**
- One solution, one price
- Simple, straightforward
- Best for commodity services

**Options Estimate (Recommended):**
- Multiple tiers (Good, Better, Best)
- Customer chooses based on budget and needs
- Higher close rate than single option
- Increases average ticket

**Example: AC Replacement Estimate**

**Option 1 - Essential ($8,500):**
- 2-ton standard efficiency unit (14 SEER)
- Standard installation
- 5-year parts warranty
- Basic thermostat

**Option 2 - Preferred ($11,200) ⭐ RECOMMENDED:**
- 2-ton high efficiency unit (16 SEER)
- Premium installation with upgraded materials
- 10-year parts and labor warranty
- Programmable WiFi thermostat
- Annual maintenance included (1 year)

**Option 3 - Ultimate ($14,800):**
- 2-ton ultra-high efficiency unit (18 SEER)
- Premium installation with all upgrades
- Lifetime parts and labor warranty
- Smart thermostat with zoning
- Annual maintenance included (5 years)
- Indoor air quality package

**Why This Works:**
- Addresses different budgets
- Provides clear differentiation
- Customer feels in control
- Increases perceived value
- Higher average sale

## Presenting & Selling

### Presentation Best Practices

**Preparation:**
- Know your customer's needs and concerns
- Understand property details and history
- Have supporting materials ready (photos, specs)
- Be confident in your recommendations

**Setting the Stage:**
- Find quiet, comfortable environment
- Minimize distractions
- Have estimate displayed professionally
- Customer can easily see and read

**Presentation Flow:**

1. **Recap the Situation**
   - "Here's what we found during our inspection..."
   - Show photos and evidence
   - Explain the problem clearly
   - Discuss implications if not addressed

2. **Present Solutions**
   - "We've prepared three options for you..."
   - Start with recommended (usually "Better")
   - Explain each option's benefits
   - Highlight differences between tiers

3. **Emphasize Value, Not Price**
   - Focus on benefits and outcomes
   - Energy savings, comfort, reliability
   - Warranty and peace of mind
   - Long-term cost of ownership

4. **Use Visual Aids**
   - Photos from inspection
   - Manufacturer brochures
   - Comparison charts
   - Customer reviews/testimonials

5. **Address Questions & Concerns**
   - Listen actively
   - Answer honestly and clearly
   - Overcome objections professionally
   - Provide additional information as needed

6. **Ask for the Decision**
   - "Which option works best for you?"
   - Don't pressure, but don't be passive
   - Offer financing if available
   - Discuss scheduling and next steps

7. **Get Approval & Signature**
   - Customer selects option
   - Review and sign estimate
   - Collect deposit if required
   - Schedule work

### Overcoming Common Objections

**"It's too expensive"**
- **Response:** Focus on value and ROI
- Explain financing options
- Compare to cost of not fixing (emergency repairs, energy waste)
- Offer lower-tier option if appropriate

**"I need to think about it"**
- **Response:** "What concerns do you have?"
- Address specific hesitations
- Offer limited-time incentive if applicable
- Schedule follow-up call/visit

**"I want to get other quotes"**
- **Response:** "That's smart, and we encourage it."
- Highlight your unique value (warranty, service, reputation)
- Provide testimonials and references
- "We're confident you'll find our proposal competitive"

**"Can you do it cheaper?"**
- **Response:** "Let's review what's included..."
- Explain value of each component
- Offer lower-tier option with reduced features
- Stand firm on quality and value
- Avoid cutting corners that compromise quality

**"Not right now / Maybe later"**
- **Response:** Understand timing concerns
- Explain consequences of delay (if applicable)
- Offer flexible scheduling
- Set up follow-up reminder
- Stay in touch with nurture campaigns

## Estimate Conversion

### Converting Estimates to Jobs

**When Customer Approves Estimate:**

1. **In ServiceTitan:**
   - Open the estimate
   - Click **"Sold"** or **"Convert to Job"**
   - Estimate status changes to "Sold"

2. **Job Automatically Created:**
   - New job generated from estimate
   - All services/materials transferred
   - Customer and location linked

3. **Schedule the Work:**
   - Coordinate with dispatch
   - Assign technician(s)
   - Set appointment date/time
   - Notify customer

4. **Collect Deposit (if required):**
   - Process deposit payment
   - Applied to final invoice
   - Documented in system

5. **Work Performed:**
   - Technician completes job
   - Job marked complete
   - Invoice generated
   - Final payment collected

### Following Up on Pending Estimates

**Estimate Statuses:**
- **Draft:** Being created, not yet presented
- **Presented:** Shown to customer, awaiting decision
- **Sold:** Approved by customer, converting to job
- **Dismissed:** Customer declined
- **Follow-Up:** Needs additional contact

**Follow-Up Best Practices:**

**Timing:**
- **24 hours:** Quick check-in call
- **3 days:** If no response, call again
- **1 week:** Final outreach, offer expiration if applicable
- **Ongoing:** Add to nurture campaign for future opportunities

**Follow-Up Script:**
"Hi [Customer Name], this is [Your Name] from [Company]. I wanted to follow up on the estimate we provided for [service]. Do you have any questions I can answer? Is there anything holding you back from moving forward?"

**Handling Responses:**
- **Questions:** Answer thoroughly, address concerns
- **Need more time:** Set specific follow-up date
- **Declined:** Ask why (learn for future), thank them
- **Approved:** Schedule work immediately

### Estimate Analytics

**Track Key Metrics:**
- **Estimates Created:** Volume of quotes generated
- **Presentation Rate:** % of estimates presented to customers
- **Close Rate:** % of presented estimates sold
- **Average Estimate Value:** Mean dollar amount
- **Time to Close:** Days from presentation to approval
- **Dismissed Reasons:** Why estimates are declined

**Use Data to Improve:**
- Identify high-performing services
- Refine pricing strategies
- Target training needs
- Optimize presentation techniques

## Pricing Strategies

### Setting Competitive Prices

**Factors to Consider:**
- **Your costs:** Labor, materials, overhead
- **Market rates:** What competitors charge
- **Value provided:** Quality, service, warranty
- **Target margins:** Desired profitability
- **Customer perception:** Premium vs. value brand

**Pricing Models:**

**1. Cost-Plus Pricing:**
- Calculate total cost
- Add desired markup percentage
- Simple, ensures profitability
- May not reflect market value

**2. Market-Based Pricing:**
- Research competitor pricing
- Position relative to market
- Adjust based on differentiation
- Responsive to market conditions

**3. Value-Based Pricing:**
- Price based on customer value received
- Higher prices for premium service
- Justify with quality, warranty, expertise
- Can maximize margins

**4. Flat-Rate Pricing:**
- Fixed price per service/job type
- Predictable for customers
- Simplifies quoting
- Popular in home services

### Margin Management

**Understanding Margins:**
- **Cost:** What you pay for labor, materials, overhead
- **Price:** What customer pays
- **Gross Margin:** (Price - Cost) / Price × 100%

**Target Margins (Industry Typical):**
- **Materials:** 50-100% markup
- **Labor:** 30-50% margin
- **Equipment:** 20-40% margin
- **Overall:** 40-60% gross margin

**Improving Margins:**
- Negotiate better supplier pricing
- Reduce labor inefficiency
- Upsell premium services
- Minimize discounting
- Increase operational efficiency

### Discounting Strategies

**When to Discount:**
- **Volume/Multi-Service:** Reward larger purchases
- **Membership/Loyalty:** Incentivize recurring relationships
- **Seasonal Promotions:** Boost slow periods
- **Competitive Matching:** Stay competitive when necessary
- **Manager Approval:** Case-by-case exceptions

**When NOT to Discount:**
- **Routinely:** Erodes margin and perceived value
- **Without reason:** Seems arbitrary or desperate
- **Excessively:** Customers question original pricing
- **Untracked:** Lose visibility into margin impact

**Best Practices:**
- Set discount limits by role
- Require approval for large discounts
- Track discount impact on margins
- Use strategically, not habitually

## Sales Performance Tracking

### Key Sales Metrics

**Individual Performance:**
- **Estimates Created:** Quantity of quotes
- **Conversion Rate:** % of estimates sold
- **Average Ticket:** Mean sale value
- **Total Revenue:** Dollar volume sold
- **Sold Hours:** Billable hours generated

**Team Performance:**
- **Department conversion rate**
- **Revenue by business unit**
- **Top performers (leaderboard)**
- **Trends over time**

### Accessing Sales Reports

**In ServiceTitan:**

1. **Navigate to Reports**
   - **Reports > Sales Reports**

2. **Common Sales Reports:**
   - **Estimates Report:** All estimates with statuses
   - **Sold Estimates Report:** Only converted estimates
   - **Technician Scorecard:** Individual performance metrics
   - **Sales Leaderboard:** Top performers ranking
   - **Estimate Follow-Up Report:** Pending estimates needing attention

3. **Customize Reports:**
   - Filter by date range, business unit, technician
   - Export to Excel for further analysis
   - Schedule automated delivery

**Analyze Patterns:**
- **High conversion services:** Promote and train on these
- **Low conversion services:** Refine pricing or presentation
- **Top performers:** Learn their techniques, share with team
- **Dismissed reasons:** Address common objections

**Set Goals:**
- **Individual targets:** Revenue, conversion rate, average ticket
- **Team goals:** Monthly/quarterly objectives
- **Incentives:** Commissions, bonuses, recognition

**Regular Reviews:**
- **Daily:** Quick check on day's performance
- **Weekly:** Review metrics, identify issues
- **Monthly:** Comprehensive analysis, adjust strategies
- **Quarterly:** Strategic planning, goal setting

## Best Practices

### Sales Excellence

**Do's:**
- ✅ **Listen first:** Understand customer needs before presenting
- ✅ **Educate customers:** Explain options and benefits clearly
- ✅ **Offer choices:** Good-better-best, not single option
- ✅ **Focus on value:** Benefits and outcomes, not just features
- ✅ **Be consultative:** Advise, don't just sell
- ✅ **Follow up promptly:** Don't let estimates go stale
- ✅ **Track metrics:** Know your numbers, improve continuously

**Don'ts:**
- ❌ **High-pressure tactics:** Coercion damages reputation
- ❌ **Oversell:** Don't recommend unnecessary work
- ❌ **Ignore objections:** Address concerns honestly
- ❌ **Discount reflexively:** Preserve value and margins
- ❌ **Forget follow-up:** Persistence pays off
- ❌ **Wing it:** Prepare and present professionally
- ❌ **Take rejection personally:** Learn and move on

### Estimate Quality

**Professional Estimates Include:**
- Clear, detailed descriptions
- Itemized pricing (not just total)
- Multiple options when appropriate
- Photos and documentation
- Warranty information
- Company branding and professionalism
- Terms and conditions
- Expiration date (if applicable)

**Avoid:**
- Vague descriptions
- Hidden fees or surprise charges
- Typos and errors
- Ugly or unprofessional formatting
- Missing contact information
- Lack of differentiation between options

## Troubleshooting

### Common Sales Issues

**Low Conversion Rate:**

**Symptoms:** Many estimates created, few sold

**Solutions:**
- Review presentation techniques (use Sales Pro if available)
- Analyze dismissed reasons
- Check pricing competitiveness
- Improve estimate quality and clarity
- Provide more customer options
- Enhance follow-up process
- Train on objection handling

**Estimates Not Converting to Jobs:**

**Symptoms:** Estimates marked "Sold" but jobs not created

**Solutions:**
- Verify estimate is marked "Sold" (not just "Presented")
- Check permissions (user may not have conversion rights)
- Manually convert: Open estimate, click "Convert to Job"
- Contact support if system issue

**Pricebook Items Missing:**

**Symptoms:** Can't find services/materials when building estimate

**Solutions:**
- Check filters (business unit, category)
- Search by SKU or partial name
- Verify item is "Active" (not archived)
- Check user permissions
- Contact admin to add missing items

**Pricing Errors:**

**Symptoms:** Wrong prices showing for services/materials

**Solutions:**
- Verify pricebook item pricing
- Check for dynamic pricing rules affecting price
- Review client-specific pricing overrides
- Ensure estimate is using correct business unit pricebook
